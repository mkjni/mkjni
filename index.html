Resume Guide for Non-IT and IT Candidates

For Non-IT Candidates

  1. Name: Clearly state your full name at the top.
  2. Contact Number: Provide a valid phone number where you can be easily reached.
  3. Email Address: Use an email address you actively use.
  4. Location: City and State/Union Territory name where you are residing currently.
  5. Links (Optional but Preferred):
    • LinkedIn Profile
    • Personal Portfolio
    • GitHub
  6. Experience:
    • Job Title
    • Company Name
    • Company Website (optional but preferred)
    • About the company services/products:
    • Company Location (optional)
    • Start Date - End Date (In DD/MM/YYYY or MM/YYYY format)
  7. Role and Responsibilities:
    • Write 3-4 points showcasing the relevant experience you have as per the job description, using the exact keywords mentioned in the job description.
    • Write the rest of the relevant experience you have as per the role.
  8. Education:
    • Name of the Qualification: Your degree or certification.
    • Start Date - End Date (In DD/MM/YYYY or MM/YYYY format): Duration of the course.
    • Name of the School/College/Institution: The name of the institution where you studied.
    • Grade/Percentage: Your academic performance.
  9. Certificates:
    • Name of the Certification - Issued by: Include the name and issuing organization.
  10. Skills: Relevant skills you have as per the job description and relevant skills as per the role. (It needs to be a word and not a sentence.)
  11. Technical Skills: Mention software you have used in your job, like CRM, ERP, SAP, MS Excel. (It needs to be a word and not a sentence.)
  12. Languages Known: (Mandatory for BPO roles) Name of the language: Read, Write, and Speak.

Final Notes:

  • Customize it according to the job: Different jobs have different requirements. Therefore, read the job description, and make sure your resume has what is needed for that specific job.
  • Use relevant keywords: If your resume has the keywords that a job description requires, it becomes very easy for the Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to select it.
  • Keep it clean and short: Try to limit it to 1-3 pages. This makes you look professional, and it becomes easy for employers to go through your resume.
  • Proofread: Spelling or grammatical errors are a turn-off to employers. Therefore, once you have finished making your resume, do read it once, or get it read by someone else.
